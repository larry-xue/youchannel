# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

YouChannel speaks to open-source readers, language-learning builders, AI product
designers, and future maintainers who want to understand what was built and what
was learned. They are usually evaluating the project from a browser or GitHub,
not trying to complete an in-app workflow.

## Product Purpose

YouChannel was an experiment in turning YouTube videos into interactive language
practice: import playlists, analyze videos, chat with the material, practice
speaking live, and track progress. The static site now serves as a clear public
archive for the project: what it did, why it existed, how it was built, and why
it was ultimately paused instead of turned into a product business.

## Brand Personality

Reflective, precise, and maker-led. The tone should feel candid and technical
without becoming cold. It should read like a thoughtful build note from a team
that learned something useful.

## Anti-references

Avoid hype-heavy AI landing pages, fake growth claims, vague productivity
language, and generic SaaS card grids. Do not present the project as an active
commercial product if the real message is an open-source archive and postmortem.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the concrete artifact: YouTube videos becoming lessons, chats,
  practice drills, and assessments.
- Explain the system honestly: browser app, Supabase, Gemini, YouTube OAuth,
  background jobs, and admin tooling.
- Separate product promise from operating reality.
- Make the stop decision legible and useful to other builders.
- Keep the page deployable as plain static HTML.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-readable content, semantic headings, useful alt
text, and reduced-motion fallbacks. The static page should not require
JavaScript to understand the story.
