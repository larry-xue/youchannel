YouChannel was an experiment in turning real YouTube videos into AI-assisted language practice: playlist import, video analysis, contextual chat, live speaking sessions, shadowing drills, and progress history.

The product idea

Authentic input needed a practice loop.

Most language apps begin with scripted lessons. YouChannel started from the opposite direction: real videos learners already cared about. The goal was to turn that messy material into structured practice without removing the texture that made it worth watching.

Library

Imported YouTube videos became a searchable learning library.

Analysis

Each video could produce summaries, timestamps, characters, and transcript data.

Live

Gemini Live powered real-time voice practice around a learner profile.

Shadowing

Practice drills turned recent conversations into repeatable speaking exercises.

How it was built

A serious prototype, not a mockup.

The repository includes a TanStack Start app, Supabase schema, YouTube OAuth, background jobs, admin tooling, quota tables, and a documented OpenAPI analysis endpoint. The system worked as a product-shaped experiment, which is exactly why its operating costs became visible.

TanStack StartUser app and routes.
SupabaseAuth, Postgres, RLS, quotas.
YouTube APIOAuth and playlist import.
GeminiVideo analysis and live voice.
Jobs serviceFastify, pg-boss, admin console.

The decision

We stopped before pretending it was simple.

The product direction was compelling, but the shape was too expensive and too dependent on external platforms for the stage it was in. Continuing would have meant building a business around quota design, support, model cost, and content uncertainty instead of the learning loop itself.

What worked

Real video material created richer practice than canned lesson scripts. Live conversation, shadowing, and learner memory pointed toward a useful product.

What did not scale

Video analysis was costly, live voice was sensitive to model behavior, YouTube access created platform risk, and the backend needed operational care beyond a small experiment.
